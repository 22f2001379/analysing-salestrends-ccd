# Optimising Café Coffee Day (IIT Madras) Sales Through Data Analytics

A real-world business analytics project focused on understanding customer demand, revenue drivers, and inventory requirements at the Café Coffee Day (CCD) outlet inside the Indian Institute of Technology Madras (IITM).

Using approximately 90 days of sales data collected directly from the outlet, this project identifies high-performing products, customer purchasing patterns, sales trends, and inventory requirements to support data-driven operational decisions.

## Project Overview

The CCD outlet at IIT Madras operates in a unique environment with subsidised pricing and a highly diverse customer base consisting of students, faculty, researchers, and staff.

The objective of this project was to answer key business questions:

* Which products generate the majority of revenue?
* How do sales vary across time?
* Are food and beverage purchases correlated?
* Which inventory items require increased stocking?
* How can operational efficiency and profitability be improved?

The project combines descriptive analytics, statistical analysis, forecasting, and business intelligence techniques to generate actionable recommendations.

## Business Impact

This analysis revealed several important findings:

* Over 80% of revenue is generated by a small subset of products.
* Food contributes nearly as much revenue as beverages.
* Weekend demand significantly exceeds weekday demand.
* Evening hours experience the highest customer traffic.
* Certain products face substantial inventory shortages relative to forecasted demand.
* Strategic combo offers present opportunities for increasing average order value.

These insights can support:

* Inventory optimisation
* Revenue growth
* Waste reduction
* Improved staffing decisions
* Better product prioritisation

## Methodology

### 1. Descriptive Analytics

Used to understand:

* Revenue distribution
* Sales variability
* Product performance
* Quantity trends

### 2. Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)

Identified the small set of products responsible for the majority of revenue.

Business Value:

* Inventory prioritisation
* Product visibility optimisation
* Menu rationalisation

### 3. Time Series Analysis

Examined:

* Daily sales behaviour
* Weekend effects
* Demand fluctuations

Business Value:

* Workforce planning
* Inventory scheduling
* Peak-hour preparation

### 4. Pearson Correlation Analysis

Measured relationships between snack and beverage purchases.

Business Value:

* Combo offer design
* Cross-selling opportunities

### 5. Forecasting

Implemented:

* Holt-Winters Exponential Smoothing
* Simple Moving Average (SMA)

Evaluation Metrics:

* MAE
* MAPE
* R² Score

Business Value:

* Demand forecasting
* Inventory planning
* Stockout prevention

## Key Findings

### Revenue Concentration

A small group of products contributes the majority of outlet revenue.

Top performers include:

* Tandoori Chicken Sandwich
* Gourmet Belgian
* Spinach & Corn Sandwich
* Café Frappe
* Cappuccino
* Devil's Own

### Customer Behaviour

* Weekends consistently outperform weekdays
* Evening hours generate the highest transaction volume
* Demand patterns remain relatively stable over time

### Product Mix

Contrary to expectations:

* Beverages contribute ~53% of revenue
* Food contributes ~47% of revenue

The outlet functions as both a café and a quick-service food provider.

### Cross-Selling Potential

Snack and beverage sales exhibit a weak positive relationship.

Potential opportunity:

* Beverage + snack combo offers
* Targeted promotional bundles

### Inventory Forecasting

Products requiring substantial inventory increases include:

* TA Iced Mocha
* Cheese
* Tandoori Chicken
* Classic Garlic
* Chilli Cheese
